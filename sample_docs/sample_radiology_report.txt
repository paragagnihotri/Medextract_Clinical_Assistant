FINDINGS

LIVER:
The liver is normal in size and demonstrates smooth contours. The liver parenchyma shows
heterogeneous attenuation. A well-defined hypodense lesion is noted in Segment VI of the right
hepatic lobe, measuring 3.1 cm x 2.8 cm in axial dimensions. The lesion demonstrates peripheral
nodular enhancement on arterial phase with progressive fill-in on portal venous and delayed phases,
consistent with haemangioma. No washout. No satellite lesions. No biliary radicle dilatation.

A second smaller lesion of 0.8 cm is noted in Segment II of the left hepatic lobe, too small to
characterise definitively. No arterial enhancement. Likely a small cyst — recommend follow-up.

GALLBLADDER AND BILIARY SYSTEM:
The gallbladder is distended. Multiple calculi are seen within the gallbladder lumen, the largest
measuring 1.5 cm. The gallbladder wall shows mild thickening of 4 mm, consistent with chronic
cholecystitis. No pericholecystic fluid. No signs of acute cholecystitis.
The common bile duct measures 6 mm in diameter (upper limit of normal). No choledocholithiasis.
No intrahepatic bile duct dilatation.

PANCREAS:
The pancreas is normal in size, morphology, and signal intensity. No focal pancreatic lesion.
No pancreatic duct dilatation. No peripancreatic fluid or fat stranding.

SPLEEN:

2. INDETERMINATE SMALL HEPATIC LESION (Segment II, left lobe) — 0.8 cm. Too small to characterise.

4. SIMPLE RIGHT RENAL CYST — 1.3 cm, Bosniak Category I. No further workup required.

5. MILD LUMBAR SPONDYLOSIS at L3-L4 and L4-L5. Incidental finding.

6. No hepatic malignancy identified. No biliary obstruction. No peritoneal disease.

1. MRI Liver with hepatocyte-specific contrast (Primovist) within 4 weeks — to further characterise

2. Surgical referral for symptomatic cholelithiasis — laparoscopic cholecystectomy should be
   considered given chronic cholecystitis and recurrent symptoms.

3. Routine follow-up imaging for the right renal cyst is NOT required (Bosniak I).

4. Orthopaedic or physiotherapy referral for symptomatic lumbar spondylosis if clinically indicated.
